What you should be able to do after this lecture

  • Explain the alignment problem as a proxy-objective mismatch.
  • Identify common failure modes (Goodhart, distribution shift, reward hacking).
  • Relate alignment to governance constraints (fairness, robustness, safety, compliance).
  • Interpret alignment as a constraint on behavior, not just model parameters.

Potential Questions for In-Class Quiz

Short-answer (7-10 minutes each). Justify claims.

Quiz 1 — Goodhart’s Law Conceptual + Applied

A performance metric M(h) is used as a proxy for human utility U(h).

Prompt: Explain why optimizing M(h) aggressively may reduce U(h).

  • Your answer must reference distribution shift or gaming behavior.
  • Your answer must distinguish between correlation and objective equivalence.

Q2 — Behavioral formulation Short answer

A deployed system induces a probability law μ ∈ P(Ω). Why is it more principled to impose alignment constraints on μ rather than directly on internal parameters θ?

Q3 — Sociotechnical framing Short answer

Provide one reason why alignment cannot be solved purely as a technical optimization problem.

Quiz 4 — Feasible Set Geometry Reasoning

Consider the constrained formulation: minh L(h) subject to Ci(h) ≤ 0.

Prompt: Suppose a fairness constraint shrinks the feasible region. What happens to the optimal value of L(h) after adding constraints?

Choose one and justify:

  1. It must improve
  2. It must worsen or stay the same
  3. It is unaffected
  4. It becomes undefined

Quiz 5 — Parameter vs Behavioral Alignment Governance

Two models h1 and h2 have different internal parameters but induce the same distribution over trajectories μ.

Prompt: From a governance perspective, are they meaningfully different? Why or why not?

  • Your answer should reference observability and accountability.
  • Optional: mention non-identifiability or “different parameters, same behavior.”
Tip: distinguish between performance metrics, normative objectives, and institutional constraints.
