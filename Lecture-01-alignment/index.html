What you should be able to do after this lecture

  • Explain the alignment problem as a proxy-objective mismatch.
  • Identify common failure modes (Goodhart, distribution shift, reward hacking).
  • Relate alignment to governance constraints (fairness, robustness, safety, compliance).
  • Interpret alignment as a constraint on behavior, not just model parameters.

In-Class Quiz

Short-answer (7-10 minutes each). Justify claims briefly.

Q1 — Alignment vs generalization T/F + justification

True or False: If a model generalizes well on held-out test data, then it is aligned.

Q2 — Behavioral formulation Short answer

A deployed system induces a probability law μ ∈ P(Ω). Why is it more principled to impose alignment constraints on μ rather than directly on internal parameters θ?

Q3 — Sociotechnical framing Short answer

Provide one reason why alignment cannot be solved purely as a technical optimization problem.

Tip: distinguish between performance metrics, normative objectives, and institutional constraints.
